FizzBuzz is a classic programming problem. Here's the rule:

> For numbers 1 to N, print:
> - **"Fizz"** if the number is divisible by 3
> - **"Buzz"** if the number is divisible by 5
> - **"FizzBuzz"** if divisible by **both** 3 and 5
> - The **number itself** if none of the above apply

So for 1–20 it looks like:

```
1, 2, Fizz, 4, Buzz, Fizz, 7, 8, Fizz, Buzz,
11, Fizz, 13, 14, FizzBuzz, 16, 17, Fizz, 19, Buzz
```

It originated as a children's counting game, then became a staple coding interview question in the early 2000s because it's simple enough to explain in 30 seconds but still requires you to think about conditions and logic. It's perfect for TDD intro because the rules are small and learnable one at a time — which is exactly why your course uses it on Day 1/3.
